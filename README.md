Cosmic  
======
A framework for inferring condition-specific microRNA-mediated ceRNA modules  
Background  
----------
Breast cancer is a highly heterogeneous malignant tumor, and its development involves multi-level regulatory dysregulation at the genetic, epigenetic, transcriptional, and post-transcriptional levels. Competing endogenous RNA (ceRNA) plays a crucial role in breast cancer, widely participating in malignant processes such as breast cancer cell proliferation, invasion, metastasis, and immune evasion. However, existing research lacks systematic identification and functional analysis of breast cancer subtypes and tissue-specific ceRNA modules, making it difficult to fully reveal the heterogeneous regulatory patterns of breast cancer. Therefore, this study, from a systems biology perspective, constructs a comprehensive analytical framework, Cosmic (Condition-specific microRNA-mediated ceRNA modules), for the identification and functional analysis of breast cancer condition-specific ceRNA modules, systematically inferring and analyzing breast cancer subtypes and tissue-specific ceRNA modules.  
The main workflow of the Cosmic framework can be divided into four parts. The first part is the collection and preprocessing of breast cancer subtype and tissue heterogeneity data; the second part is the inference of breast cancer subtype-specific ceRNA modules; the third part is the inference of breast cancer tissue-specific ceRNA modules; and the fourth part is the functional analysis of breast cancer subtype and tissue-specific ceRNA modules.
